Overview

In previous labs you should have finished the connections for the RSC, excluding the placing of the PLDs. This is the final lab for the semester. You should finish wiring the RSC and load memory with the three programs you wrote in the previous lab to demonstrate that your computer executes the programs correctly.
This lab is worth 100 points, so make sure it works properly.

This lab is due at the specified time (04/27/2018 @ 18:00). Late labs will not be accepted. NO EXCEPTIONS! There is only one submission for this lab!

Procedure

  1. Add your PLDs to the Control Unit and finish wiring the RSC.
  2. From your OUTR register, use a splitter and add eight hexadecimal displays to allow the output to be seen in Hex rather than Binary. If your program uses ASCII in some form, you may use a TTY display. To do this you will need to split off the 7 least significant bits to run into the input of the TTY. Also you will need to run the OUTR_LD signal to the load pin on the TTY. Don't forget to connect the clock signal also.
  3. This step is optional, although very useful for debugging. Add probes to which ever lines you would like to visually monitor while the computer is running. The nice thing about probes is that it will automatically conform to whatever bit width the line you are attaching it to is. For example, if you want to monitor what value is in the PC register, you could put a probe on the PC_OUT, and now you will be able to monitor the PC value as the computer is running. It might be especially helpful to monitor the values of the ACC and R register, or anywhere you feel it is necessary.
  4. Test your RSC thoroughly! Make absolutely certain that it works with all of your programs (which should work in your computer if it works in the emulator!) A tip for testing your RSC circuit: start with the RAM1 file, as it doesn't do very much. Load this program into your RAM module and then manually tick your Clock (Ctrl+T or the finger tool) and convince yourself that the appropriate control lines coming out of your control unit are active at the correct combination of T-values, D-values, and the Clock's state. If something doesn't work properly, double-check your PLD wiring in your control unit in addition to double-checking your expressions if you suspect the wiring is correct. If you have trouble pinpointing a problem, ASK QUESTIONS NOW rather than later! Don't wait until an hour before the project is due, because chances are if there's one issue, there will be others. :)
  5. Print off a screenshot of your RSC by going to File -> Export Image in Logisim. Find the lab instructor to demo your computer. You will have to demo all three RAM files you created. Make sure you explain what your RAM3 does before the demo. Once you have demonstrated that your computer is working properly, the instructor will initial your printed screenshot. Once your RSC screenshot has been signed off on, submit your entire project (RSC.circ, PLD.circ, custom_reg.circ, all three RAM files, etc.) to the OLA 9 dropbox. Any submission to the OLA 9 dropbox that is not signed off will not be accepted.

    6. It is strongly encouraged that you sign up for a time to due your demo.

Requirements

  • Computer must be fully functional. All instructions must work as intended.
  • You must run the above simulations under the supervision of the lab instructor. Make sure they initial off on your printed page that the RSC ran.

Submission

  • A zipped file containing:
    • RSC.circ and any other circuit files needed for the RSC (e.g. PLD.circ and custom_reg.circ)
    • All three RAM files
